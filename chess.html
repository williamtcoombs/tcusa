0. Blueprint

Spend sixty percent of your time on endgame and middle game, leaving forty percent for openings. Focus on a few openings that are inherently sound structurally, are not as dependent on move order, and where there is less memorization required. As White, consider the London System. As Black, pick a response to 1.e4 (e.g., Petrov Defense, Caro-Kann Defense, French Defense) and something for 1.d4 (e.g., Slav Defense, King's Indian Defense).

9. Flank Openings

A flank opening is typified by play on one or both flanks, the portion of the chess board outside the central d and e files.


A. The English Opening

1.c4 is the fourth most popular opening and is considered reliable and flexible. White begins the fight for the centre by staking a claim to the d5-square from the wing, in hypermodern style. This opening is often used as a transpositional device in much the same way as 1.Nf3 - to avoid such highly regarded responses to 1.d4 as the Nimzo-Indian and Grünfeld Defense.
